Upon successful completion of the course, students should understand:  
1) the structure and function of neurons and basic neurophysiology; 
2) basic neuroanatomy; 
3)  the anatomy and function of autonomic, endocrine, motor, and sensory systems; 
4)  the evolutionary continuum between species and the evolution of behavior; 
5) the neurobiological bases of fundamental behaviors such as feeding, reproduction, defense, circadian rhythms, learning, etc.; 
6) the neurobiological bases for psychopathology.
