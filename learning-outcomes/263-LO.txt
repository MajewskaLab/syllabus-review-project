Course Objectives and Goals:
• To identify and compare general theory, site formation processes, basic archaeological fieldtechniques, and the disciplines that contribute to zooarchaeology, particularly as they applyto zoological evidence of human/environmental relationships.
• To identify and compare strengths and weaknesses in the materials, methods, andtechniques used in zooarchaeology.
• To identify and compare the contributions zooarchaeology makes to studies of therelationships between humans and their environments and to the studies of currentenvironmental issues by documenting historical trends.
• To practice professional level research skills needed to do lab-based research inzooarchaeology. These skills include:identification of mammal, bird, reptile, amphibian, and fish remainsquantification of archaeofaunal remains using common zooarchaeological methods; interpretation related to human behavior and environmental relationships.
