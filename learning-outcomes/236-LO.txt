By the end of the course students should have learned:
•	Some of the historical, philosophical, and methodological issues associated with behavioral neuroscience
•	Some of the methods used in the study of behavioral neuroscience
•	Some of the fundamentals of neuroscience
•	Some general and recent knowledge regarding behavioral neuroscience
•	Some general and recent knowledge regarding study of animal behavior
•	The laboratory component of this course will provide the student with in depth exposure to basic neuroanatomy and comparative animal behavior, and will illustrate principles of sensory processing that supplement material presented in class
