By the end of the course, students should be able to:
 
describe the major classes of biological molecules, identify them in foods, and understand why they are required in our diets and bodies.

characterize cells as the basic units of life and understand how they perform the major functions required for life, including energy transfer, growth, and reproduction.
 
understand the genetic continuity of life, including the nature and transmission of genes and manipulation of genes in modern DNA technology.
 
understand how evolution provides the unifying principle of Biology which explains how accumulated genetic change accounts for the huge diversity of life on earth.

understand the basis of ecology and the impact of humans on the environment.
